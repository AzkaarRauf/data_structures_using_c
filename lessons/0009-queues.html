Queues (FIFO)

12th-April, stated plainly: "There will be no direct question on the Q in your exam… you can't expect any direct question on the programming." So this is concept + tracing only — but a trace-the-output MCQ is likely. Low effort, easy marks.

1. The model (FIFO)

  • FIFO — First In, First Out. Like people in a line: first to arrive is first served.
  • Two ends: rear = where you insert, front = where you remove.
  • enqueue = insert (at rear). dequeue = remove (from front).
Contrast with a stack: a stack is LIFO and uses one end (top). A queue is FIFO and uses two ends (front + rear).

2. How front & rear move

Both pointers only ever increment: enqueue increases rear, dequeue increases front (the dequeued element is left behind, front moves past it).

Trace: enqueue 10, 20, 30; dequeue; enqueue 40

OperationfrontrearQueue (front→rear)
enqueue 10101010
enqueue 20102010 20
enqueue 30103010 20 30
dequeue203020 30  (removed 10)
enqueue 40204020 30 40

First out was 10 — the first one in. That's FIFO.

3. Variants (SLM — names only)

VariantIdea
Circular queueRear wraps back to index 0 to reuse freed front slots.
Priority queueElements served by priority, not arrival order.
DequeDouble-ended — insert/remove at both ends.

4. Scoring in the exam

No full queue program will be asked. Expect: a random enqueue/dequeue sequence → "what is the output?" or "what's at the front now?" Trace it row by row like the table above. Also know the stack-vs-queue one-liner: LIFO/one-end vs FIFO/two-ends.
